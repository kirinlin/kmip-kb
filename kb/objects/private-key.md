# Private Key

## Purpose

A Private Key is a managed cryptographic object holding the private half of an
asymmetric key pair — the sensitive secret used to sign or to decrypt material
encrypted to its public partner. Private keys are typically generated by
[Create Key Pair](../operations/create-key-pair.md) or supplied with
[Register](../operations/register.md).

## Structure

| Field | Tag | XML Element | Required | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Key Block | `420040` | `KeyBlock` | Yes | The [Key Block](../ttlv/key-block.md) carrying the private key's format, algorithm, length, and material. |

Because the material is sensitive, the Key Block is frequently transported in
wrapped form per a [Key Wrapping Specification](../ttlv/key-wrapping-specification.md)
when retrieved with [Get](../operations/get.md).

## Key Attributes

A private key's defining attributes are
[Cryptographic Algorithm](../attributes/cryptographic-algorithm.md),
[Cryptographic Length](../attributes/cryptographic-length.md), and a
[Cryptographic Usage Mask](../attributes/cryptographic-usage-mask.md) set for
private-key operations such as sign or decrypt. The server assigns a
[Unique Identifier](../attributes/unique-identifier.md), maintains a
[Link](../attributes/link.md) of type Public Key to the partner
[Public Key](public-key.md), and tracks its [State](../attributes/state.md).

## Lifecycle & State

A private key follows the managed-object lifecycle via its
[State](../attributes/state.md). It is created alongside, and cross-linked to,
its public partner. Given its sensitivity, access control and wrapped transport
matter throughout its life; [Revoke](../operations/revoke.md) and
[Destroy](../operations/destroy.md) retire it.
